Agnieszka (‘Aga’) Borowiec

"My adventure with massage therapies started in 2015, when I took an Introduction to Massage Course in the School of Holistic Therapy, Edinburgh. At the end of the course, my tutor who was quite pleased with my performance, asked if I would like to take a course in Holistic Therapeutic Massage."

The following ten months, which was the duration of the course, were simply great . Always looking forward to the next class and learning plenty of massage pain-relieving techniques, including myofascial release, neuromuscular techniques and stretching. In the meantime, I have discovered my inner potential and the importance of listening and recognising physical and emotional states in the people around me.

After obtaining my qualifications, my Tutor, Simonetta, offered me a job as Teaching Assistant in her school and I have been working there ever since.

Craving for more skills and techniques, I have taken an Advanced Myofascial Release in the same School. This massage is incredibly effective, not only on a physical but also an emotional level. In the last years I have become very specialised in Myofascial Release.

Hugh Gilbert is another important teacher I had the pleasure to learn from. He is the developer of Kinetic Chain Release, a system of gentle stretches and joint mobilisations that brings the body back into balance. In 2018 I became an accredited practitioner of KCR.

2018 was truly unforgettable. I’ve travelled to Chiangmai in Thailand to learn Traditional Thai Massage from one of the most renown masters, Homprang Chaleekanha, the director at Baan Hom Samunphari. This experience of in-depth body work has made me very confident and showed me a new way of creating a sense of ease and well-being.

This is all so far, but the world of massage is vast, and the next adventure awaits. I’ll keep you posted.

Aga
